<p><p>The telecom tower market comprises two primary types: tower structures and mast structures. Tower structures are typically taller and built from steel, designed for heavy antenna loads, providing stability and space for multiple service providers. In contrast, mast structures are usually lighter and slender, suitable for areas where space is limited, often supporting fewer antennas. These structures facilitate wireless communication by hosting equipment essential for cellular networks, contributing to improved connectivity and coverage across urban and rural regions.</p></p>
