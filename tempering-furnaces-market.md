<p><p>Tempering furnaces are crucial in heat treatment processes, and they come in two main types: batch and continuous. Batch-type furnaces process large quantities of materials in discrete batches, offering flexibility for varied product sizes and compositions. In contrast, continuous-type furnaces maintain a constant flow of materials, optimizing throughput and efficiency for high-volume production. The choice between these types depends on the specific production requirements, including scale, material characteristics, and desired heating uniformity in the tempering process.</p></p>
