<p><p>The Poly Lactic Acid (PLA) market is segmented into three main types: Poly(L-lactic) acid (PLLA), which is derived solely from L-lactic acid and features high strength and crystallinity; Poly(D-lactic) acid (PDLA), made from D-lactic acid, known for its superior thermal properties; and Poly(DL-lactic) acid (PDLLA), a racemic mixture that balances the properties of both PLLA and PDLA, offering flexibility and varied degradation rates. Each type caters to specific applications in industries like packaging, textiles, and biomedical engineering.</p></p>
