Touch-based systems require physical contact, enabling drivers and passengers to interact with interfaces, such as touchscreens or panels, for navigation, climate control, or entertainment. In contrast, touchless systems detect hand movements or gestures without physical contact, enhancing safety and convenience by allowing users to operate features through air gestures. Both systems aim to improve user experience while minimizing distractions and promoting intuitive interaction within vehicles.</p></p>
