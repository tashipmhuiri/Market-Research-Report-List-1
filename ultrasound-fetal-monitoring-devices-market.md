<p><p>Ultrasound fetal monitoring devices are categorized into several types based on imaging technology. 2D ultrasound provides a flat, two-dimensional view of the fetus, aiding in basic assessments. 3D ultrasound enhances this by creating a three-dimensional image, offering more detailed visualizations. 4D ultrasound adds motion to 3D images, allowing for real-time visualization of the fetus’s movements. Doppler imaging focuses on blood flow and can assess the fetus’s heart rate and health by evaluating blood circulation, enhancing prenatal care and monitoring.</p></p>
