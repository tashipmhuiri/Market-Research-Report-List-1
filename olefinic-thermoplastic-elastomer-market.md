<p><p>The olefinic thermoplastic elastomer market primarily consists of two types: Thermoplastic Polyolefin (TPO) and Thermoplastic Vulcanizates (TPV). TPO is a blend of polypropylene and rubber, offering a balance of flexibility, durability, and resistance to chemicals, making it suitable for automotive and construction applications. TPV, on the other hand, is created by dynamically vulcanizing rubber within a polyolefin matrix. This results in enhanced elasticity and resilience, ideal for applications requiring superior performance, such as in automotive seals and gaskets.</p></p>
