Grade A nickel oxide is characterized by high purity and is primarily used in advanced electronics, such as semiconductor manufacturing and lithium-ion batteries. Grade F, while still of good quality, has slightly lower purity levels and is typically utilized in more general applications within electronics, including conductive inks and coatings.
