<p><p>Thermal plasterboards are specialized building materials designed to enhance the thermal insulation of walls and ceilings. They consist of a gypsum core laminated with insulating materials, such as expanded polystyrene (EPS) or mineral wool, which help reduce heat transfer and improve energy efficiency in buildings. These boards are widely used in residential, commercial, and industrial construction to meet energy-saving regulations and reduce heating and cooling costs.</p><p>The Thermal Plasterboards Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing demand for energy-efficient construction materials and stricter building regulations aimed at reducing carbon emissions. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.9% during the forecast period. This growth is fueled by rising awareness of sustainable building practices, urbanization, and the need for retrofitting older buildings with modern insulation solutions.</p><p>Key trends in the market include the development of lightweight and fire-resistant thermal plasterboards, as well as the integration of advanced materials like aerogel for superior insulation performance. Additionally, the adoption of green building certifications, such as LEED and BREEAM, is encouraging the use of thermal plasterboards in construction projects. Emerging markets in Asia-Pacific and the Middle East are also contributing to the market's expansion due to rapid infrastructure development and increasing construction activities.</p></p>

<p><p>Thermal plasterboards are specialized building materials designed to enhance insulation and energy efficiency in walls and ceilings. They come in two main market types: Tapered-Edged and Square-Edged. Tapered-Edged plasterboards have beveled edges, making them ideal for seamless jointing and smooth finishes, commonly used in ceilings and walls. Square-Edged plasterboards have straight edges, suitable for applications requiring robust, flat surfaces, often used in drylining or areas where additional finishing layers are applied. Both types cater to specific construction needs, balancing thermal performance and installation requirements.</p></p>
