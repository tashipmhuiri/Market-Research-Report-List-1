<p><p>In the conductive glass substrate market, the primary types include FTO (Fluorine-doped Tin Oxide), ITO (Indium Tin Oxide), and other variants. FTO substrates are known for their excellent conductivity and chemical stability, often used in solar cells and displays. ITO substrates offer a high transmittance and are widely used in touchscreens and OLEDs. Other types may include materials like silver nanowire and graphene-based substrates, which provide alternative solutions for specific applications, enhancing performance and functionality across various industries.</p></p>
