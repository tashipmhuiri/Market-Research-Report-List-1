<p><p>The influenza antiviral drugs market encompasses several types, each targeting different mechanisms of the virus. Penetration and dehulling inhibitors prevent the virus from entering host cells. DNA polymerase inhibitors disrupt viral genetic replication. Reverse transcriptase inhibitors target the enzyme necessary for converting viral RNA into DNA. Protein inhibitors prevent the assembly of viral proteins, while neuraminidase inhibitors block the release of new viral particles. Broad-spectrum antiviral drugs offer efficacy against multiple viruses, enhancing treatment options for influenza and related infections.</p></p>
