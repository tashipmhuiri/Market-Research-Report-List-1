<p><p>The monosaccharide market is categorized based on the number of carbon atoms in the sugar molecule. Triose (three carbons) and tetrose (four carbons) sugars are less common but serve as intermediates in metabolic pathways. Pentose sugars (five carbons), such as ribose and xylose, play critical roles in nucleic acids and energy metabolism. Hexose sugars (six carbons), including glucose and fructose, are the most abundant and widely utilized for energy, making them the primary focus of the market.</p></p>
